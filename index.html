Cumulative Education Reflection

How has ISU prepared me to become a better engineer?

ISU has helped me become a better engineer by granting me the opportunity to take many practical group project classes that exposed me to design of larger software systems. Often, these highly relevant projects necessitated the formulation of requirements specifications, while considering the potential impact of the project in an ethical way.

In class projects & problem solving tasks, did you draw upon information, research, or experiences beyond what was provided in class to successfully complete your work?

There have been many instances where I was required to consult materials from different sources. Over the past 2 decades there has been an explosion of emerging frameworks, libraries and technologies. Utilizing these available resources required the consideration of licenses, and a quick grasp of API documentation.

How did learning activities outside of the classroom help you to understand the importance of Lifelong Learning?

I participated in several extra-curricular activities to complement my educational experience at ISU. In particular, I really enjoyed exploring new technology at coding events such as Hackathon's or Iowa State's famous Cyber Defense Competition (CDC). These helped me better appreciate what it is like working with other software engineers that may have different backgrounds but similar interests to me.

Have you started to undertake any new learning to improve your ability to apply skills or knowledge to new problems and to develop confidence in taking risks?

Two of my big interests are natural language processing and machine learning. During my summer time, I used my free time to explore these two fields by creating some of my own conversational agents. My next project is fit my Raspberry Pi with a custom home intrusion detection kit that I build. These projects may not necessarily be related to my classes, but that makes them even more exciting!

If you were to do your undergraduate work again, what things would you change?

One area of improvement I see at ISU is its math education. It concerns me that many math classes are not very rigorous and heavily calculation-based (by omission of theory). I think math is the basis of innovation in any science, and will play an increasingly important role with the rise of AI and Data Science in the coming years.

What are your current short term (within next one or two years) and long-term (next five to ten years) goals?

My current short term goals is to find a full-time technical job. It is my goal to become a better engineer by becoming aware of modern industry practices upon leaving the classroom, and entering the work force. My long-term goal is to become familiar with a specific domain so that I can take on a more business-oriented role. What have you learned in your general education electives beyond the technical aspects in formulating engineering solutions?

The most important thing I learned in my general education electives was how to work together with students from other majors. Engineering and computer science in general is interdisciplinary in nature, hence this is something I will be faced with very frequently in future. Another important thing I learned is how to effective communicate across the WOVE (Written, Oral, Visual, Electronic) spectrum. In particular, the fundamentals of public speaking class was very helpful in teaching me how to present and better articulate myself when speaking in front of a foreign audience.

What impact does meshing technical engineering skills and general electives-based innovation have in an economic, global, or societal context?

One time I used skills from general electives skills was during hackathons. Generally in hackathons, one spends 1-2 days to implement a new idea, or explore a new technology in a team of 4. After this, each team holds a short pitch on the idea that they implemented in order to win prices. Certainly, the skills I mentioned in the response above have helped make this a more pleasant experience for everyone. In a societal context, these skills I gained from general education electives are invaluable in pinning down important details in a precise manner, to develop software that is safe, reliable, and with the intended functionalities.
